Energy

AI is fundamentally energy-hungry. Training a single large model can consume as much electricity as hundreds of homes use in a year. Inference at scale multiplies this demand. Energy is the binding constraint on AI's growth trajectory.

~1,300 MWh
Energy to train GPT-4 — equivalent to ~120 US homes' annual consumption
10×
Inference energy can exceed training energy over a model's deployed lifetime
~4%
Projected share of global electricity demand from data centers by 2030 (IEA)
1 GW+
Power capacity planned for individual frontier AI data centers

Key Dynamics

  • Power is the bottleneck. Grid interconnection delays in the US can exceed 5 years for new data center projects
  • Location matters. AI data centers cluster near cheap, reliable power — hydro in the PNW, nuclear in Virginia, renewables in Texas
  • Cooling innovation. Liquid cooling and immersion cooling are becoming standard as chip densities rise beyond 1 kW per rack
  • Nuclear revival. Microsoft (Three Mile Island), Amazon (Talen), and Google (Kairos) are signing nuclear power deals for AI
  • Carbon impact. AI's carbon footprint is under increasing scrutiny — driving demand for 24/7 carbon-free energy matching
  • Sustainability vs. speed. Tension between net-zero commitments and the race to deploy AI capacity

⚡ Implications for Companies

Energy constraints will shape AI strategy. Companies must factor power availability into data center location decisions, monitor the carbon intensity of their AI workloads, and prepare for rising energy costs as a material AI operating expense.

The Competitive Landscape

N

NVIDIA

H100, B200, Blackwell — the dominant AI GPU platform. Software moat via CUDA ecosystem.

G

Google (TPU)

Custom ASIC chips (Trillium 6th gen). Cloud-only availability. Competitive perf/watt.

A

Amazon (Trainium / Inferentia)

Purpose-built for AWS. Trainium2 for training, Inferentia for cost-effective inference.

M

Microsoft (Maia)

Custom AI accelerator for Azure. First-gen chip focused on inference workloads.

A

AMD (MI300X)

Competing GPU alternative. Strong inference performance. ROCm software ecosystem maturing.

💡 Implications for Companies

GPU supply is concentrated but diversifying. Companies should avoid single-vendor lock-in, monitor the CUDA-alternative software ecosystem (ROCm, Triton, OpenXLA), and treat AI compute as a strategic procurement category.

Foundation models are the brains of the stack. The frontier is advancing rapidly — with open-source models closing the gap on proprietary leaders. Model economics are shifting as inference costs drop and small models get more capable.

GPT-4o
OpenAI's multimodal flagship. Omni-modal across text, vision, and audio
Claude 4
Anthropic's frontier model. Emphasis on safety, long context (200K tokens), and reasoning
Gemini 2.5
Google's most capable model. 1M+ token context window. Strong multimodal performance
Llama 4
Meta's open-source family. Closing the gap with proprietary models at lower cost

Key Model Dynamics

  • Open vs. Closed. Llama, Mistral, Qwen, DeepSeek lead open-source. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google lead proprietary. Open models offer control & cost; closed models lead on capability & safety
  • Small models rising. Models like Phi, Gemma, and Llama-8B deliver surprisingly strong results for enterprise tasks at a fraction of the cost
  • Multimodal is standard. Vision, audio, and video understanding are now baseline expectations
  • Agentic capabilities. Models are increasingly capable of tool use, planning, and multi-step reasoning — enabling autonomous AI agents
  • Inference cost collapse. Prices for API inference have dropped 80-90% year-over-year, democratizing access
  • Fine-tuning & RAG. Enterprise deployment patterns are solidifying: RAG for knowledge grounding, fine-tuning for specialized tasks

🧠 Implications for Companies

Model selection is now a portfolio decision — not a single-vendor bet. Evaluate models on cost, latency, accuracy, and control. Prepare for multi-model architectures. The model layer is commoditizing; differentiation shifts to data, workflows, and applications.

Applications are where value is captured. Every layer below is infrastructure cost — the application layer is where revenue, productivity, and competitive advantage materialize. Enterprise AI adoption is shifting from experimentation to deployment.

$15.7T
PwC estimate of AI's potential contribution to the global economy by 2030
72%
Organizations that adopted AI in at least one business function (McKinsey 2024)
40%
Average productivity gain reported by early enterprise AI adopters
2025+
Year of AI agents — autonomous AI systems moving from pilot to production

Use Cases by Industry

  • Drug Discovery. AI-designed drugs entering clinical trials. Protein folding (AlphaFold). Target identification at scale
  • Legal Copilots. Contract analysis, e-discovery, legal research. 80%+ time reduction on document review
  • Financial Services. Fraud detection, algorithmic trading, credit underwriting, regulatory compliance
  • Software Engineering. Code generation (GitHub Copilot, Cursor). 30-50% developer productivity gains reported
  • Customer Service. AI agents handling complex support queries. 24/7 availability. 60%+ deflection rates
  • Manufacturing. Predictive maintenance, quality inspection (computer vision), supply chain optimization

🚀 Implications for Companies

The application layer is where competitive advantage lives. Start with high-ROI, low-risk use cases. Invest in change management — technology is only 30% of the equation. Build AI fluency across the organization. The winners won't be companies with the best models — they'll be companies that best embed AI into their workflows and products.

Key Takeaways for Companies

Bringing it all together
⚡

Energy Is the Binding Constraint

AI growth is ultimately limited by power availability. Monitor energy costs, carbon intensity, and location strategy. Power procurement is now an AI strategy decision.

💻

Chip Supply Is Strategic

GPU concentration creates risk. Diversify chip sources, track the CUDA-alternative ecosystem, and treat AI compute as a board-level procurement concern.

☁️

Infrastructure Decisions Compound

Cloud choices create multi-year lock-in. Networking is as critical as GPU count. Inference will dominate compute spend — design for it from day one.

🧠

Models Are Commoditizing

Model quality is converging. Competitive advantage shifts from which model you use to how you use it — data, workflows, and applications become the differentiator.

🚀

Applications Capture the Value

The application layer delivers ROI. Start with proven use cases, invest in change management, and build AI capability across the organization, not just in technical teams.

🔗

Every Layer Is Connected

Decisions in one layer cascade through the stack. A weak foundation undermines everything above it. Take a full-stack view — energy to application — when building your AI strategy.
