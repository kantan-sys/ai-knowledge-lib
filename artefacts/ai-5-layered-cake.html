Energy

AI is energy-hungry. Training a single large model can consume as much electricity as hundreds of homes use in a year. Australia faces unique challenges — and opportunities — at the intersection of AI growth and energy infrastructure.

~1,300 MWh
Energy to train GPT-4 — equivalent to ~120 homes' annual consumption
10×
Inference energy can exceed training energy over a model's deployed lifetime
~5%
Data center electricity demand as share of Australia's NEM by 2030 (AEMO projection)
Snowy 2.0
2.2 GW pumped hydro — critical for firming renewables to power AI data centers

Australia's Energy Landscape for AI

  • AEMO & NEM. The National Electricity Market serves eastern/southern states. Grid connection delays of 2-5 years are a bottleneck for new data centers
  • Renewable abundance. Australia has among the world's best solar & wind resources — a competitive advantage for green AI infrastructure
  • Data center boom. Sydney and Melbourne are seeing record data center investment (NEXTDC, AirTrunk, Equinix, CDC)
  • Hydro & storage. Snowy 2.0 and battery storage (Hornsdale, Victoria Big Battery) are critical for 24/7 carbon-free matching
  • Transmission constraints. New interconnectors (EnergyConnect, Marinus Link) needed to move renewables to data center load centers
  • Carbon policy. Safeguard Mechanism reforms put emissions caps on large facilities — data centers will be captured

⚡ Implications for Australian Business

Energy costs and availability are now a strategic AI decision. Companies must consider data center power contracts, NEM price exposure, and the carbon intensity of AI workloads. Australia's renewable advantage is a potential draw for sovereign and green AI infrastructure.

Sovereign AI & Australian Capability

  • CSIRO Data61. Australia's national science agency. Research in NLP, computer vision, and responsible AI. Language models for Australian context
  • AustLang. Australian Government's own LLM initiative — sovereign, secure AI for government use cases
  • University research. ANU, UNSW, University of Sydney, Monash — world-class AI/ML research groups. Key talent pipeline
  • Open vs. closed. Llama, Mistral, DeepSeek for sovereign on-prem deployment. OpenAI/Anthropic via cloud for rapid innovation
  • Small models. Increasingly viable for enterprise at low cost. Key for Australian mid-market and regulated sectors
  • AI safety. Australia's AI Ethics Principles (8 voluntary principles). Potential mandatory guardrails under consultation

🧠 Implications for Australian Business

Model selection is a portfolio decision. Open-source models offer sovereign deployment and lower cost. Closed models lead on capability. For regulated industries — consider on-shore model hosting. Australia's AI ethics framework signals the direction of future regulation.

Australian Use Cases

  • Agtech. CSIRO Ag2050, AI-powered weed detection (AgriWebb), livestock monitoring, climate-resilient farming
  • Financial Services. CBA & ANZ AI fraud detection, Afterpay/PayTo risk models, compliance automation (APRA-regulated)
  • Health & MedTech. Harrison.ai (radiology), Prospection (health data analytics), Australian Digital Health Agency AI roadmap
  • GovTech. AustLang sovereign LLM, myGov AI-assisted services, ATO compliance AI, Digital ID integration
  • SaaS & Enterprise. Canva AI, Atlassian Intelligence, Employment Hero — Australian-built AI features scaling globally

🚀 Implications for Australian Business

Australia's competitive moat is domain expertise — mining, agriculture, health, and financial services. AI amplifies this. Companies should embed AI into existing products and workflows, invest in change management, and treat AI fluency as a workforce-wide capability (not just a tech team concern).

Summary

Key Takeaways — Australian Perspective 🇦🇺 AU

Bringing it all together
⚡

Energy Is the Binding Constraint

Australia's NEM grid is under growing pressure from data center demand. Our renewable energy abundance is a strategic advantage — but transmission and firming (Snowy 2.0, batteries) must keep pace with AI growth.

💻

Sovereign Capability Gap

Australia is entirely dependent on imported advanced chips. AUKUS and quantum computing research (SQC, Diraq) offer long-term strategic hedges. Short-term: manage supplier concentration risk.

☁️

Data Sovereignty Is Non-Negotiable

Government, defence, and critical infrastructure require onshore data. Hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP) serve Australian regions. Sovereign cloud (NEXTDC, AU cloud) is maturing — at a premium.

🧠

Models Are Commoditizing

Open-source models (Llama, Mistral) enable sovereign deployment. Closed models lead on capability. Australia's competitive advantage lies in domain-specific adaptation, not model building.

🚀

Build on Australian Strengths

Mining, agtech, health, fintech — sectors where Australia has global leadership. AI amplifies our domain expertise. Build AI into products and workflows, not bolt-on experiments.

🔗

Full-Stack Thinking Required

Energy → Chips → Cloud → Models → Applications. Every layer is connected. Australia's AI strategy must address the full stack — or risk critical dependencies in the layers below.
