Energy

AI is energy-hungry. Training frontier models now consumes electricity on par with small cities. Inference at scale multiplies this demand. The race for clean, reliable power has become the binding constraint on AI's growth — and Australia has both unique challenges and a renewable energy advantage.

~6%+
Projected data centre share of Australia's NEM electricity demand by 2035 (AEMO Integrated System Plan)
1 GW+
Power capacity planned for individual frontier AI data centres — equivalent to a large coal plant
Snowy 2.0
2.2 GW pumped hydro — critical for firming variable renewables to support 24/7 AI infrastructure loads
82%
Australia's renewable electricity target by 2030 — positions the country as a green AI infra destination

Australia's Energy Landscape for AI

  • AEMO & NEM. The National Electricity Market serves eastern/southern states. Grid connection delays of 2–5 years are the primary bottleneck for new data centres
  • Renewable advantage. Australia has among the world's best solar and wind resources — a structural cost advantage for 24/7 carbon-free AI infrastructure
  • Data centre investment boom. Sydney and Melbourne seeing record DC investment (NEXTDC, AirTrunk/Macquarie, Equinix, CDC, AWS, Microsoft)
  • Storage & firming. Snowy 2.0, Waratah Super Battery, and Victoria Big Battery are critical for matching intermittent renewables to 24/7 AI loads
  • Transmission build-out. EnergyConnect (SA-NSW), HumeLink, and Marinus Link are essential to move renewable power to data centre load centres
  • Safeguard Mechanism. Australia's emissions cap on large industrial facilities increasingly captures data centres — carbon compliance is now a design constraint

⚡ Implications for Australian Business

Energy availability and carbon intensity are now strategic AI decisions. Data centre location must account for NEM connection timelines and price exposure. Australia's renewable advantage can attract sovereign and green AI infrastructure — if transmission keeps pace with demand.

Competitive Landscape & Geopolitics

N

NVIDIA (H100 → B200 → Blackwell Ultra → Vera Rubin)

Blackwell launched Mar 2024. Blackwell Ultra & Vera Rubin announced GTC 2025. CUDA moat intact.

DS

DeepSeek (R1 & V3)

Chinese startup that demonstrated frontier-level AI at fraction of the cost. Triggered $600B NVIDIA rout.

G

Google TPU (Trillium, 6th gen)

Custom ASIC. Competitive perf/watt. Cloud-only. Powers Gemini training & inference.

A

Amazon Trainium2 & AMD MI300X

Custom AWS silicon + AMD GPU alternative. ROCm ecosystem maturing. Inference cost pressure.

China Export Wars (2025-2026)

  • NVIDIA designed H20 chip specifically for Chinese market to comply with US export restrictions
  • Sep 2025: China's CAC banned ByteDance, Alibaba & others from buying NVIDIA RTX Pro 6000D chips
  • NVIDIA & AMD agreed to pay 15% of China chip revenues to US government for export licenses
  • China's refusal to buy could cost NVIDIA ~$30 billion. US later permitted H200 exports under conditions (2026)

💡 Implications for Australian Business

Australia is entirely dependent on imported advanced semiconductors. AUKUS Pillar II opens trusted supply chain access. The DeepSeek moment shows models can be trained more efficiently — reducing GPU dependency. Australia's quantum computing research (SQC, Diraq, UNSW) is world-class — a long-term strategic asset as NVIDIA enters quantum AI (Apr 2026).

Key Model Dynamics & Australian Context

  • Open vs. Closed. Llama 4, Mistral, DeepSeek R1 lead open-source. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google lead proprietary. Open models now competitive on capability
  • DeepSeek's efficiency shock. Demonstrated frontier performance at fraction of cost. Challenges "more GPUs = better"
  • Agentic AI. Models now capable of tool use, planning, multi-step reasoning — enabling code agents like Cursor & Windsurf
  • CSIRO Data61 & AustLang. Australia's sovereign AI: national science agency research + government's own secure LLM
  • AI safety. Australia's 8 voluntary AI Ethics Principles. Mandatory guardrails under active consultation. EU AI Act influence growing

🧠 Implications for Australian Business

Model selection is now a portfolio decision. Open-source models (Llama, DeepSeek) enable sovereign deployment at lower cost. Closed models lead on frontier capability. DeepSeek proved efficiency matters as much as scale. For regulated industries — on-shore model hosting is becoming a compliance requirement.

Summary

Key Takeaways — Australian Perspective 🇦🇺 AU

Updated June 2026
⚡

Energy Is the Binding Constraint

Australia's NEM grid faces growing data centre demand. Our renewable abundance is a strategic asset — but transmission (EnergyConnect, HumeLink, Marinus) and firming (Snowy 2.0, batteries) must keep pace with AI growth.

💻

Chip Geopolitics Are Volatile

NVIDIA hit $5T then lost $600B in a day. DeepSeek proved efficiency can beat brute force. China export controls are reshaping supply chains. Australia must navigate AUKUS and build sovereign quantum capability.

☁️

Data Sovereignty Is Mandatory

Government, defence, and critical infrastructure require onshore data. Hyperscalers serve Australian regions. Sovereign cloud (NEXTDC, CDC, Azure AU cloud) is maturing — plan for a 20-30% cost premium.

🧠

Models Are Commoditising — Fast

DeepSeek R1 matched GPT-4 at ~5% of the cost. Open-source is closing the gap. Australia's advantage lies in domain-specific adaptation (mining, health, agtech), not model building.

🚀

Build on Australian Strengths

Mining, agtech, health, fintech — sectors where Australia leads globally. AI amplifies our domain expertise into exportable products. Embed AI into workflows, not bolt-on experiments.

🔗

Full-Stack Thinking Required

Energy → Chips → Cloud → Models → Applications. Every layer is connected. Australia's AI strategy must address the full stack — or risk critical dependencies in the layers below. Sovereign capability matters.
